## Overview / 项目概览

Mathematical modeling practice is less about finding one elegant formula and more about building a defensible chain of reasoning. I use open-ended prompts to practice defining the decision context, making assumptions, selecting variables, constructing a tractable model, and explaining limitations.

## What I practice / 训练内容

- Problem formulation and scope control
- Assumption design and variable definition
- Translating qualitative questions into quantitative relationships
- Model interpretation and sensitivity thinking
- Structured technical writing with LaTeX

## Reflection / 复盘

The most useful habit has been connecting every modeling choice back to the original question. A model can be mathematically correct and still be unhelpful if its assumptions, outputs, or uncertainty do not match the decision being made.

This page intentionally does not claim competition awards, rankings, or specific outcomes. Those details will only be added if there is a real report or result to reference.
